What is Spectra absorption?

Absorption spectra refer to the pattern of absorption of light by a substance as a function of its wavelength or frequency. When light passes through a substance, some light can be absorbed by the material, which can cause electrons in the substance's atoms or molecules to become excited and move to higher energy levels. Similarly, it is the result of the specific wavelengths of light that the substance absorbs, and these wavelengths can be used to identify the substance and determine its concentration.
