What is emission Spectra?

-Emission spectra of iron

The emission spectrum refers to the electromagnetic radiation emitted by an atom or molecule as it transitions from a high energy state to a lower energy state. Each energy transition produces photon emissions at a specific frequency corresponding to a specific wavelength. The ensemble of these photon emissions, which give rise to different wavelengths, constitutes the emission spectrum.

Each element in the periodic table has a unique emission spectrum known as a spectral signature, so that by analyzing the emission spectrum of a substance, we can identify its constituent elements. This procedure is called emission spectroscopy, which is a very useful tool in various fields of science. For example, it is used in chemical analysis and in astronomy to study the elements contained in a star.

The electromagnetic spectrum, on the other hand, is the full range of electromagnetic radiation, from low-energy radio waves to high-energy gamma rays. The electromagnetic spectrum is divided into different regions, each of which is characterized by a specific frequency range and corresponding wavelength.

On the other hand, the relationship between the emission spectrum and the electromagnetic spectrum is that photon emissions from an emission spectrum occur at a specific frequency and wavelength, which fall within a particular region of the electromagnetic spectrum.
