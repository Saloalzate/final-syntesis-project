The emission spectrum refers to the electromagnetic radiation emitted by an atom or molecule as it transitions from a high energy state to a lower energy state. Each energy transition produces photon emissions at a specific frequency corresponding to a specific wavelength. The ensemble of these photon emissions, which give rise to different wavelengths, constitutes the emission spectrum.

Each element in the periodic table has a unique emission spectrum known as a spectral signature, so that by analyzing the emission spectrum of a substance, we can identify its constituent elements.

This procedure is called emission spectroscopy, which is a very useful tool in various fields of science.
