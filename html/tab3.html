Composition of the planets and the stars

-The system solar-

The chemical elements of which our Solar System is composed are the same, and in the same proportions, as those found in other planetary systems and in stars similar to the Sun. The cloud in which our Solar System formed was composed mainly of hydrogen and helium, and only about 2% contained heavier elements. The four rocky planets formed from the heavy particles, and the four gaseous planets from the light elements.

What is the composition of the planets?

The planets of the solar system have the following composition:

TERRESTRIAL PLANETS:planets with bodies consisting largely of rocks. They consist of metallic cores surrounded by a solid rock surface. Planets such as Earth, Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars. Earth is composed of 32% oxygen, 15% silicon, 14% magnesium,3% sulfur and 2% nickel.

GIANT OR JOVIAN PLANETS: These are planets that are fundamentally larger than the terrestrial planets. Such as Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.

GAS GIANTS: They are Jupiter and Saturn, giant planets, which are composed of hydrogen and helium. They are also the largest planets in the Solar System.

ICE GIANTS: These are Uranus and Neptune, which are composed mainly of materials with a low breaking point. Such as water, methane and alkali, with thick hydrogen and helium climates. These planets are essentially lower in mass than the gas giants.

What are the stars made of?

Stars are made of very hot gas, mostly hydrogen and helium, which are the two lightest elements.

Stars shine by burning hydrogen into helium in their cores, and later in their lives they create heavier elements.

What is the composition of the sun?

It is classified as a yellow dwarf star, type G2, and all the planets of the solar system revolve around it.

The Sun is a glowing ball, a star or celestial body, made up of very hot gases, mainly hydrogen (92.1%) and helium (7.9%). There are also traces of other elements (0.1%), such as oxygen, carbon, nitrogen, silicon, magnesium, neon, iron, and sulfur. Most of the gas exists in the form of plasma.
